This guide breaks down the Ministry of Corporate Affairs (MCA) **General Circular No. 01/2026** regarding the **Companies Compliance Facilitation Scheme, 2026 (CCFS-2026)** into logical chapters and business-friendly subsections. 

Use this document to quickly assess how your company can save on penalty fees, regularize pending filings, or exit/close down with minimal financial burden.

---

# **Executive Overview: CCFS-2026 at a Glance**

*   **What is it?** A one-time relief scheme to clear backlogged compliance filings, obtain "dormant" status, or shut down a company with deeply discounted fees and immunity from legal penalties.
*   **Active Period:** **15th April 2026 to 15th July 2026** (a strict 3-month window).
*   **Who benefits?** Active companies with overdue filings, startups, MSMEs, one-person companies (OPCs), and owners looking to safely shut down defunct businesses.

---

# **Chapter 1: Scope, Applicability & Exclusions**

This chapter helps you determine if your business is eligible to participate in the CCFS-2026 scheme.

### **1.1 Covered E-Forms (The Compliances You Can Regularize)**
The scheme applies to specific delayed filings from both the **Companies Act, 2013** and the **Companies Act, 1956**:

| Act Version | Relevant E-Forms Covered under the Scheme |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Companies Act, 2013 Forms** | `MGT-7`, `MGT-7A` (Annual Returns), `AOC-4`, `AOC-4 CFS`, `AOC-4 NBFC (Ind AS)`, `AOC-4 CFS NBFC (Ind AS)`, `AOC-4 (XBRL)` (Financial Statements), `ADT-1` (Auditor Appointment), `FC-3`, `FC-4` (Foreign Company profiles/accounts). |
| **Companies Act, 1956 Forms** | `Form 20B`, `Form 21A`, `Form 23AC`, `Form 23ACA`, `Form 23AC-XBRL`, `Form 23ACA-XBRL`, `Form 66`, and `Form 23B`. |

### **1.2 Who is EXCLUDED from the Scheme?**
Your business **cannot** use this scheme if it falls into any of the following categories:
1.  **Active Strike-Off Initiated:** Companies against whom the Registrar of Companies (RoC) has already initiated final strike-off notices under Section 248.
2.  **Voluntary Strike-Off Already Filed:** Companies that have already submitted an application to be struck off before this scheme launched.
3.  **Prior Dormancy Applications:** Companies that filed for Dormant Status (under Section 455) before the start of this scheme.
4.  **Amalgamated/Dissolved Entities:** Companies dissolved because of a merger or amalgamation scheme.
5.  **Vanishing Companies:** Companies designated as "vanishing companies" by the regulatory authorities.

# **Chapter 2: Three Strategic Pathways for Your Business**

Business owners can choose one of three paths during this 3-month window depending on their operational status:

└──────────────────┘        └──────────────────┘        └──────────────────┘

### **Pathway A: Regularize Your Active Business**
*   **Best for:** Operational companies that missed filing deadlines and want to clear their record without heavy penalties.
*   **Benefit:** Complete all backlogged annual filings by paying **only 10% of the total accrued late fees** (saving 90% on penalties).

### **Pathway B: Declare the Company "Dormant"**
*   **Best for:** Inactive companies that you want to keep registered for future use (e.g., holding intellectual property or land) with minimal ongoing compliance costs.
*   **Benefit:** Apply for "Dormant Status" under Section 455 using e-form `MSC-1` at **50% of the normal filing fee**.

### **Pathway C: Permanently Close (Strike Off) the Business**
*   **Best for:** Owners of defunct or inactive companies who want to shut down operations permanently and avoid future legal liabilities.
*   **Benefit:** File for official strike-off using e-form `STK-2` and pay **only 25% of the standard closure filing fees** (saving 75%).

# **Chapter 4: Legal Protection & Immunity Clauses**

Filing under CCFS-2026 provides distinct legal shields, but the timing of your filings determines the level of protection you receive.

### **4.1 Immunity for Annual Returns & Financial Statements (Sec 92 & 137)**
If you file your backlogged annual forms under this scheme, **no penalty proceedings will be initiated**, provided you meet one of the following timelines:
*   **Scenario 1:** You file *before* an Adjudicating Officer issues any official compliance notice.
*   **Scenario 2:** You file *within 30 days* of receiving a compliance/adjudication notice.

> ⚠️ **Critical Warning:** If you file *after* the 30-day notice window has expired, or if an official penalty order has already been issued, your liability to pay the pre-existing penalties **will not change** despite using the scheme.

### **4.2 Immunity for Other Key Forms**
For other forms like `ADT-1` (Auditor Appointment) or `FC-3`/`FC-4` (Foreign Companies):
*   You are granted **immunity against prospective penal action** if you file under this scheme.
*   *Condition:* No prosecution or show-cause notice must have been initiated against your company before your filing submission.

# **Chapter 5: Post-Scheme Risks (What Happens If You Miss It?)**

The MCA has made it clear that this is a **one-time window**. Once the scheme closes on **15th July 2026**:
1.  **Immediate Enforcement:** The Registrar of Companies (RoC) will initiate strict legal action against all non-compliant companies that did not utilize the scheme.
2.  **Full Penalties:** The standard penalty rate of **₹100 per day** with no upper limit will resume and be strictly collected.
3.  **Prosecution:** Executive directors and company officers risk prosecution and disqualification for ongoing compliance defaults.

2.  **Evaluate Business Viability:** Assess if the company is actively trading. 
    *   *If yes:* Use **Pathway A** to clear up your record for a fraction of the cost.
    *   *If no, but you want to save the name/assets:* Use **Pathway B** to go dormant.
    *   *If no, and you want to close it:* Use **Pathway C** to safely shut down.
3.  **Prepare Budgets and Files:** Ensure your financial statements are audited and signed, and keep funds ready to pay the concession fees as soon as the scheme opens on **15th April 2026**. Do not wait until the final deadline of **15th July 2026** to avoid system congestion.
