To make this official circular easy to read and understand for business owners, directors, and compliance officers, the text has been organized into structured chapters and subsections. 

Each section details **how your business is affected** and **what actions you need to take** to benefit from this relief scheme.

---

# **Executive Summary: The CCFS-2026 Scheme**
The Ministry of Corporate Affairs (MCA) has introduced the **Companies Compliance Facilitation Scheme, 2026 (CCFS-2026)**. 

Normally, late filings of annual returns and financial statements incur a heavy penalty of **₹100 per day** with no upper limit. This scheme is a **one-time opportunity** (open from **April 15, 2026, to July 15, 2026**) for defaulting, inactive, or struggling businesses to either clear their backlogs at a fraction of the cost, register as a "dormant" company, or shut down permanently with minimal fees.

---

# **Chapter 1: Is Your Business Eligible?**

This chapter helps you determine whether your company can take advantage of the CCFS-2026 scheme or if you are excluded.

### **1.1 Eligible Businesses**
Generally, all active and inactive companies registered under the Companies Act, 2013 (or the older Companies Act, 1956) that have pending compliance filings are eligible. This is highly beneficial for:
*   New-age startups and MSMEs.
*   One Person Companies (OPCs) and Producer Companies.
*   Closely-held private companies with delayed filing histories.

### **1.2 Ineligible Businesses (Exclusions)**
Your business **cannot** use this scheme if it falls into any of the following categories:
*   **Active Strike-Off Initiated:** Companies against whom the Registrar of Companies (RoC) has already initiated final strike-off notices under Section 248.
*   **Self-Initiated Strike-Off:** Companies that have already submitted an application to be struck off.
*   **Prior Dormancy Application:** Companies that already applied for "Dormant Status" under Section 455 before this scheme started.
*   **Amalgamated/Dissolved Companies:** Companies dissolved as part of a merger or amalgamation scheme.
*   **Vanishing Companies:** Companies that have ceased filing and whose directors are untraceable.

### **Option A: Clear Pending Backlogs (Get Fully Compliant)**
*   **What it means:** If your business is active but you fell behind on your annual filings, you can clear all past-due filings.
*   **Business Impact:** You only pay **10% of the total accumulated late/additional fees**. The remaining 90% is waived.

### **Option B: Put Your Business on Hold (Go "Dormant")**
*   **What it means:** If your business is currently inactive but you want to retain the corporate entity (e.g., to hold intellectual property or future-use assets) with minimal maintenance costs.
*   **Business Impact:** You can apply for "Dormant" status (using Form **MSC-1**) and pay only **50% (half) of the normal filing fee**.

### **Option C: Shut Down the Business Permanently (Strike-Off)**
*   **What it means:** If your business is defunct and you want to close it down cleanly without paying massive penalties.
*   **Business Impact:** You can apply to strike off your company (using Form **STK-2**) by paying only **25% of the standard filing fee**.

# **Chapter 5: Legal Protection & Prosecution Immunity**

Filing late documents normally triggers prosecution and heavy penalties on both the **company** and its **directors/officers in default**. The scheme provides critical legal immunity under specific conditions:

### **5.1 Immunity for Annual Returns & Financial Statements (MGT-7 & AOC-4)**
*   **Case 1 (No Notice Received):** If you file before the Adjudicating Officer issues a notice, **no penalty will be levied**, and all proceedings are concluded.
*   **Case 2 (Notice Received):** If you have already received a notice but file under this scheme **within 30 days** of that notice, **no penalty will be levied**.
*   **Case 3 (Late / Post-Adjudication):** If 30 days have already passed since you received a notice, or if an order imposing a penalty has already been passed, **you do not get immunity from the penalty amount**, although you can still file the documents with the discounted 10% additional fee.

### **5.2 Immunity for Other Key Forms (e.g., ADT-1, FC-3, FC-4)**
You will receive complete immunity from future legal action on delayed filings for these forms *only if*:
1.  The forms are successfully filed within the active scheme window (April 15 to July 15, 2026).
2.  No formal prosecution or show-cause notices have been initiated against your company for these specific delays before you file.

# **Chapter 6: The Danger of Non-Participation**

**What happens if your business does not utilize this scheme?**

If your business is currently in default and you choose *not* to use this 3-month window:
1.  **Immediate Penalty Accrual:** The standard penalty of **₹100/day** will continue to accumulate without any ceiling limit.
2.  **Targeted Legal Action:** The circular explicitly states that once the scheme closes on July 15, 2026, **the Registrars of Companies (RoC) will take strict legal action** (prosecution and financial penalties) against all defaulting companies and directors who chose not to clean up their records.
